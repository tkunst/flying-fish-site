The Book

A magnificent pink fish
who refuses to stay in the water

One ordinary morning, the birds are going about their business in the purple sky — when something extraordinary appears. A fish. Flying. Pink. With polka dots like paws.

Told in irresistible anapestic verse, A Fish! A Fish! A Flying Fish! is a joyful, read-aloud celebration of wonder, courage, and the kind of surprise that makes everyone want to dance.

The birds tumble. The crowd gathers. And the fish — magnificent, unapologetic — soars, lands, bows, and sings.

  • Ages 3–6
  • 32 pages, self-ended picture book
  • Read-aloud friendly — rhythmic, singable verse
  • Text finalized · Illustration in progress

The Poem

A Fish! A Fish! A Flying Fish!

Written in anapestic trimeter — three beats per line, playful and propulsive. Built to be read aloud fast, slow, and loud.

Opening

One day as I looked out
across the purple sky
the birds were out and about
tickling clouds as they flew by!

Fish Appears

Then a star, and a wish,
and then flew by a fish…
Fish, you say?
Why yes, fish I say!

A fish! A fish! A flying fish!

Confused Birds

The birds were all quite confused,
as they tumbled from the sky,
watching the fish as she cruised
across the sky nearby.

Bash & Bish

With a bash and a bish
and a sound that went squish,
the birds cheered with a smile
as they made quite the pile —

A fish! A fish! A flying fish!

Glorious Fish

A glorious fish, I say!
Her scales so bright you blink,
she brightens up our day —
magnificent in pink.

The Finale

A fish who flies!
A fish who flies!
A fish who sings!
A fish who sings!

A fish! A fish! A flying singing fish!

About the Author

Trisha Kunst

TK

Trisha Kunst

Author · Data Scientist · Storyteller

Trisha Kunst writes picture books that move — in rhythm, in feeling, and (occasionally) through the air. A Fish! A Fish! A Flying Fish! is her debut picture book, written in the belief that the best read-alouds are the ones kids ask for again and again until you know every word by heart.

When she's not writing in verse, Trisha works in data science and AI, which means she spends a lot of time thinking about patterns — the kind in data, and the kind in poetry. She suspects they're more similar than they look.

She lives with her family and is currently seeking an illustrator for this project.
